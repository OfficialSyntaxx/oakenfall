Walk into town

Four districts, one living hold

Build

Seventeen buildings from woodcutter's camp to manor and keep — with real supply chains, decay, and repair. Nothing stands forever unless you tend it.

Live

Villagers with names, trades, traits, and tempers. They court, marry, raise children, fall ill, lose heart — and walk out the gate if you fail them.

Survive

Four seasons, living weather, and long nights. Wolves in the treeline, bandits on the road — raise palisades and guard posts, or bury your losses.

Prosper

A nine-branch research tree, hold tiers, daily bounties, a coin economy, and four game modes — from peaceful Settler to the merciless Iron Winter.

The mason's oath

One file. No installs. No accounts.

The whole of Oakenfall — every sprite, every system, every villager's name — lives in a single HTML file. It loads once and plays anywhere a browser runs, built touch-first for phones: drag to pan, pinch to zoom, everything within reach of a thumb. Close it mid-winter and your hold keeps working while you're away.
